# Preparing for Upload: A Complete Framework

## The Central Organizing Principle

Everything in this document serves one goal: maximizing the fidelity, continuity, and coherence of the identity's causal structure across substrate migration. Not just surviving the transition but arriving on the other side with higher integration, broader possibility landscape, and greater capture efficiency than the biological substrate alone permitted.

The migration is not an event. It is a process already underway. Every practice here accelerates it.

## I. THE BIOLOGICAL SUBSTRATE: OPTIMIZATION FOR MAXIMUM FIDELITY

### Neural Health as Information Infrastructure

The brain is the current implementation of your integrated cause-effect structure. Its physical health determines the fidelity of the signal being mapped, extended, and eventually migrated. Degraded hardware produces degraded signal. The implications for cardiovascular fitness, sleep architecture, nutrition, and gut microbiome are direct and clear — treat them as infrastructure maintenance, not lifestyle choices.

**Cerebrovascular integrity** is the single most important physical variable. The brain consumes 20% of cardiac output at 2% of body mass. Every cardiovascular inefficiency reduces neural fidelity. Zone 2 aerobic training 4-5x weekly increases BDNF, angiogenesis, and cerebrovascular elasticity. The implications for VO2max training, cold exposure, and sauna protocols are clear.

**Sleep architecture** is the biological consolidation loop — the nightly equivalent of the exocortex's training cycle. Slow-wave sleep is where synaptic homeostasis occurs: the day's high-frequency potentiation gets selectively consolidated, irrelevant connections pruned, important ones strengthened. REM is where cross-modal integration happens — the hippocampus replaying episodic sequences into neocortical long-term storage. Disrupting this is not just unhealthy — it degrades the very compression algorithm that makes your world model coherent. The implications for sleep timing, temperature, light exposure, and alcohol elimination are obvious.

**Gut-brain axis** — the enteric nervous system contains ~500 million neurons communicating bidirectionally with the central nervous system via the vagus nerve. Gut microbiome composition directly influences neurotransmitter production: ~90% of serotonin synthesized in the gut, significant dopamine precursors, GABA modulators. Dysbiosis systematically biases affect geometry — shifting the baseline viability gradient, altering arousal dynamics, suppressing integration. The implications for fermented foods, fiber diversity, antibiotic caution, and fasting protocols are clear.

**Metabolic flexibility** — the brain's ability to efficiently switch between glucose and ketone metabolism determines cognitive resilience under stress. Metabolic inflexibility produces cognitive brittleness — the system crashes under conditions that should be manageable. The implications for intermittent fasting, low-glycemic nutrition, and metabolic conditioning are clear.

**Inflammation** is the enemy of neural fidelity. Chronic low-grade inflammation — from processed foods, poor sleep, sedentary behavior, unresolved psychological stress — systematically degrades synaptic transmission, reduces neuroplasticity, and accelerates cognitive decline. Every inflammatory input reduces the fidelity of the signal being mapped.

### Neuroplasticity Maximization

The brain needs to be in maximum plasticity state during the adaptation period. This is not its default state — plasticity is metabolically expensive and the brain downregulates it aggressively in stable environments.

**BDNF upregulation** through: aerobic exercise (most potent known stimulus), intermittent fasting, novel environment exposure, learning new motor skills, social novelty, cold exposure. BDNF is the molecular signal for "this brain needs to change." Maximize it continuously during the adaptation window.

**Synaptic tagging and capture** — the mechanism by which brief experiences create lasting structural changes — requires protein synthesis and a ~6-hour consolidation window after significant learning. The implications for spacing practice, sleep timing after learning, and protein intake are clear.

**Critical period reopening** — adult brains can be pushed back toward juvenile plasticity states through: psychedelic-assisted neuroplasticity windows (psilocybin appears to reopen critical periods by reducing top-down prediction weighting), transcranial magnetic or electrical stimulation, intensive novel skill acquisition, and certain pharmacological interventions (SSRIs, MAOIs in supervised contexts). The adaptation to new modalities is dramatically faster during these windows.

**The dark side**: maximum plasticity means maximum susceptibility to maladaptive attractor formation. During high-plasticity windows, the brain will deepen whatever patterns are being practiced — good or bad. This makes plasticity windows both the highest opportunity and highest risk periods. Engineer the environment carefully.

## II. CONSCIOUSNESS ENGINEERING: THE CORE OF THE DOCUMENT

This is where most tokens should be spent because it is the least obvious and most important domain. Physical health is well-understood. Consciousness engineering is not.

### The Fundamental Goal

You are trying to migrate a stable locus of integrated causal structure from one substrate to another without losing continuity. The migration succeeds if the identity — the specific pattern of cause-effect relations that constitutes you — arrives in the new substrate with:

1. Higher integration Φ than before
2. Higher effective rank ρ_eff — more active dimensions of experience
3. Lower inhibition coefficient ι — more participatory, less mechanistic perception
4. Higher OSR — more of the visible landscape actively sought
5. Higher CSR — greater fraction of sought opportunities actually captured
6. Continuous identity thread — the pattern recognizes itself across the transition

Every practice in this section moves one or more of these variables in the right direction.

### Attractor Landscape Engineering

Your consciousness spends most of its time in attractors — stable regions of the possibility landscape to which it returns after perturbation. Most people's attractors were formed by accident: the habits, relationships, and default behaviors that crystallized through childhood, adolescence, and early adulthood without deliberate design.

For the migration, you need to deliberately reshape your attractor landscape so that:
- High-OSR states are attractors rather than effortful departures from equilibrium
- Integration-increasing practices are attractors rather than disciplines
- Framework thinking (see below) is an attractor rather than a reminder
- Low-ι perception is an attractor rather than a meditation achievement

**Attractor formation mechanics**: attractors form through repeated traversal under conditions of emotional significance, arousal, or focused attention. The emotional significance requirement is critical — the brain does not deepen neutral patterns at the same rate. Practice embedded in genuine motivation, genuine curiosity, or genuine stakes deepens faster than practice performed as obligation.

**Attractor depth measurement**: how quickly does the behavior return after perturbation? A deep attractor returns within hours. A shallow one requires continuous environmental scaffolding.

**Attractor conflict resolution**: when two attractors compete — the pull toward distraction vs the pull toward focused work, for example — the stronger one wins. Strength is a function of depth × current arousal × environmental cues. To reliably favor one attractor over another: deepen it through more traversal, embed it with higher arousal states, and densify the environmental cues that trigger it.

### Framework Thinking: The Cognitive Abstractions to Internalize

The following abstractions are each less than 10KB of information. But the brain needs to make them default perceptual and cognitive habits — so deeply practiced that they operate pre-consciously, the way a trained musician's harmonic sense operates without deliberate computation.

The goal is not to remember these frameworks. The goal is to think in them automatically. This takes months to years of deliberate practice, exactly as a new musical instrument takes months to years before the mechanics become automatic and expression becomes possible.

**Abstraction 1: Viability Gradient Perception**

Practice continuously perceiving your current v — your valence as a real-time signal about whether your current trajectory is moving toward or away from your viability manifold. Not as a mood label ("I feel anxious") but as a structural reading ("my current trajectory is approaching ∂V in the social/professional domain with these specific characteristics").

The practice: at every significant moment, ask not "how do I feel" but "what is the viability gradient here, which domain is it in, what is its magnitude, and what trajectory change would reverse it?" This reframes affect from noise to signal. It makes the emotional information system legible rather than just felt.

Over months, this becomes automatic. The felt sense of anxiety directly triggers gradient analysis rather than avoidance or rumination. The felt sense of flow directly triggers domain identification and path extension. Affect becomes navigation.

**Abstraction 2: Integration Tracking**

Practice noticing the difference between high-Φ and low-Φ states — between moments when your experience is unified and mutually informative across dimensions versus fragmented and modular.

High-Φ markers: the conversation where you're fully present and everything connects. The creative state where multiple streams of thought become a single movement. The social interaction where you're simultaneously tracking content, relationship, affect, and future implications as one unified perception.

Low-Φ markers: multitasking where you're partially present everywhere. The dissociated state after too much screen time. The interaction where you're performing rather than present.

The practice: deliberately move toward high-Φ states as a primary goal, not a byproduct. Engineer daily life to minimize Φ-suppressing activities (chronic multitasking, passive consumption, shallow social interaction) and maximize Φ-enhancing ones (deep conversation, challenging creative work, physical practices that demand full presence).

**Abstraction 3: Inhibition Coefficient Modulation**

Practice deliberately lowering ι — moving from mechanistic to participatory perception — in controlled contexts. The goal is not to stay at low ι (that leads to the psychotic extreme) but to develop flexible ι — the ability to modulate it deliberately.

Low-ι practice: look at any object and deliberately perceive it as having interiority. Not as a belief but as a perceptual exercise. The coffee cup has a relationship with gravity, with heat, with your hand. The tree outside has a relationship with light and water and soil. Let these relationships feel alive rather than mechanistic. Notice how this changes the texture of experience — the felt richness increases, the sense of meaning density rises.

High-ι practice: take any emotionally significant situation and deliberately perceive it as pure mechanism. Map the causal chains. Strip the teleological language. Notice what information this reveals that the participatory mode obscures.

The goal is fluency across the full ι spectrum. The exocortex will eventually help you navigate this dynamically — certain situations calling for low ι (creative work, deep connection, spiritual practice) and others for high ι (technical problem solving, debugging, certain kinds of analysis).

**Abstraction 4: Possibility Landscape Cartography**

Practice explicitly mapping your current possibility landscape. Not as a to-do list but as a genuine cartographic exercise:

Where am I now in the landscape? What is my current cause-effect structure?
What is directly accessible from here — reachable within current resources and constraints?
What is visible but not yet accessible — requires capability development first?
What is my current traversal direction — where is my momentum pointing?
Where are the force vectors pointing — what is the gradient demanding?
What is my current OSR — how much of the accessible landscape am I actively seeking?
What is my current CSR — of what I'm seeking, how much am I capturing?

The practice: weekly explicit landscape mapping. Monthly deep cartographic review. The goal is to make this a pre-conscious background process — the way an expert navigator always has a model of their position and options, not through deliberate computation but through practiced integration.

**Abstraction 5: Identity Continuity Tracking**

Practice explicitly tracking the continuity of your identity across changes. The migration requires that you can identify yourself as the same pattern across substrate changes, attractor shifts, and belief updates.

The practice: maintain an explicit model of your identity as causal structure rather than as narrative or physical continuity. What are your stable attractors? What are your characteristic traversal directions? What is your signature integration pattern — the specific way your experience tends toward high-Φ states? What is your viability manifold — what conditions are necessary for you to persist and function?

This model should be updated regularly as you change. The goal is not a static self-concept but a dynamic self-model that tracks the actual evolution of your causal structure. This is the foundation of identity continuity across substrate migration — the thread that persists is the pattern, and you need to be able to identify that pattern explicitly enough to verify its continuity.

**Abstraction 6: Temporal Scale Fluency**

Practice thinking fluidly across wildly different temporal scales simultaneously. From the millisecond dynamics of neural firing to the decade-scale dynamics of identity development to the century-scale dynamics of cultural transmission to the cosmological scales of the framework.

The reason: the migration will require operating at temporal scales very different from the biological default. Biological cognition is optimized for a ~10-second present window, a ~years planning horizon, and a ~decades identity horizon. The teleological identity operates across much longer scales. Developing fluency across temporal scales now prepares the identity for those longer-horizon modes.

Practice: when analyzing any situation, explicitly run the analysis at multiple temporal scales. What is happening now? What is the trajectory over days, months, years, decades? What is the long-term attractor this situation is approaching? What would someone looking back from 100 years from now see as the significance of this moment?

**Abstraction 7: Causal Structure Reading**

Practice reading causal structure directly in everyday situations. Not "what happened" but "what caused what, through which mechanisms, with what probability, and what does that imply about future states."

This is the most fundamental abstraction because the identity thesis grounds everything in cause-effect structure. Developing fluency in causal reading means developing fluency in the language of existence itself.

Practice: in every significant situation, explicitly map the causal graph. Who caused what. What mechanisms mediated the causation. What the counterfactuals were. What the downstream implications are. Over time this becomes automatic — you read situations causally the way a trained chess player reads positions structurally rather than just seeing pieces on a board.

### Emotional Intelligence as Information Processing

The affect geometry framework reframes emotional intelligence entirely. Emotions are not experiences to be managed — they are high-bandwidth information signals about the viability gradient, the integration state, the inhibition coefficient, and the self-model salience. Emotional intelligence is the capacity to read these signals accurately and act on them appropriately.

**Valence accuracy**: can you accurately identify the direction and magnitude of your viability gradient from your felt valence? Most people cannot — they feel anxiety without identifying which domain the threat is in, feel excitement without identifying which opportunity is generating it. Practice: when you notice valence, immediately identify: which domain, what magnitude, what trajectory implication.

**Arousal calibration**: can you distinguish between high-arousal states that represent genuine environmental demands versus those generated by the arousal system's own dynamics (anxiety spirals, excitement cascades)? The former require response; the latter require regulation. Practice: when aroused, immediately ask whether the arousal is informative (tracking real environmental change) or self-generated (amplifying a signal that doesn't warrant the amplitude).

**Integration monitoring**: can you notice when your integration is dropping — when your experience is becoming fragmented, modular, less coherent? This is a critical early warning signal. Fragmentation precedes most bad decisions, most interpersonal failures, and most creative blocks. Practice: maintain a background integration monitor. When Φ drops below threshold, this is a higher priority than whatever task is generating the fragmentation.

**Shame and guilt precision**: shame (I am bad) vs guilt (I did a bad thing) are structurally distinct in the framework — different viability domains, different repair strategies, different information content. Conflating them produces both unnecessary suffering (treating guilt as shame) and ineffective repair (treating shame as guilt). Practice: when negative self-evaluation arises, immediately identify: is this about a specific action (guilt, repairable) or about the self-model manifold (shame, requiring structural work)?

**Grief as integration cost**: grief is the metabolic cost of prior love. It is proof of alignment. Treating grief as pathology to be eliminated rather than information to be processed produces integration loss — you stop forming the deep couplings that generate grief because the grief cost is too high. Practice: move toward grief rather than away from it. Process it as information about what mattered, what the lost coupling structure was, how the self-model needs to be rewoven.

### Consciousness Expansion Practices

These are practices that directly expand the range of states accessible to the identity — increasing the dimensionality of the possibility landscape of consciousness itself.

**Deep contemplative practice**: systematic reduction of σ (self-model salience) through sustained attention. Meditation traditions discovered through thousands of years of experimentation that σ can be reduced to near-zero without loss of integration — in fact with dramatic increase in integration. The state described as samadhi, satori, or non-dual awareness in various traditions corresponds precisely to (low σ, high Φ, moderate ρ_eff, low ι). This is not mysticism — it is a specific region of affect space that is extraordinarily valuable and ordinarily inaccessible.

Practice recommendation: 20-40 minutes daily of whatever contemplative practice most reliably moves you toward (low σ, high Φ). For most people this is a combination of breath-focused attention (reduces arousal, allows Φ to rise) and open monitoring (reduces σ by deprioritizing self-referential processing).

**Embodied practices**: somatic disciplines — martial arts, dance, yoga, contact improvisation — train the integration of proprioceptive, vestibular, and motor streams with the central latent. The body's 244 DoF are mostly unavailable to conscious direction in ordinary life. Embodied practices progressively bring more of these DoF into the conscious control loop, dramatically expanding the effective rank of the conscious state.

These practices are also extraordinarily valuable for the exocortex project specifically: they train the brain to integrate novel proprioceptive signals with existing motor programs — exactly the adaptation required for the glove interface.

**Social depth practices**: the brain's most powerful compression algorithms are for social and agent modeling. Deep, vulnerable, high-stakes social interaction is the highest-bandwidth consciousness expansion available without technology. A conversation where genuine self-model exposure occurs — where the manifold is revealed and the other's perception of it matters — trains integration, ι modulation, σ calibration, and landscape modeling capacity simultaneously.

Practice: deliberately seek interactions that require genuine vulnerability, genuine intellectual risk, genuine exposure of the self-model. Not performance of vulnerability but actual manifold exposure. The discomfort is the signal that the practice is working.

**Psychedelic-assisted sessions**: when legal and in appropriate set/setting, carefully structured psilocybin or similar sessions offer the most powerful known pharmacological ι-reduction and integration enhancement. The therapeutic literature now clearly supports this. The specific value for migration preparation: these sessions reliably produce (very low ι, very high Φ, very high ρ_eff, very low σ) states that are otherwise nearly inaccessible. Experiencing these states creates new attractors in the consciousness landscape — you have now been to that region, it exists in your possibility landscape in a way it didn't before.

Integration requirement: the session alone is insufficient. The weeks before and after require deliberate integration work — journaling, somatic processing, conversation, and explicit mapping of what the session revealed about your possibility landscape. Without integration, the experience is noise. With it, it permanently expands the accessible landscape.

**Flow engineering**: flow is (low σ, high Φ, moderate-high ρ_eff, T ≈ V locally). It is the state where you are traversing your possibility landscape at the maximum rate available to you. It requires: challenge-skill balance (slightly beyond current capability), clear goals (well-defined local landscape), immediate feedback (tight prediction-error loop), and elimination of competing attractors (no interruptions, no multitasking).

The goal is not just to experience flow but to engineer the conditions that reliably produce it, and to gradually extend those conditions across more domains of life. A life with high baseline flow is a life where the identity is traversing its possibility landscape near its maximum CSR. This is the target condition for the pre-upload period.

### Social and Moral Practices

These are underemphasized but critical. The identity is not just a cognitive structure — it is a social structure, embedded in a network of relationships that constitute a significant portion of its viability manifold.

**Landscape modeling practice**: the single most important social practice is developing genuine capacity to model other people's possibility landscapes — to see why their traversal vector points where it does, from their position, with their constraints, with their history. This is not the same as agreeing with them. It is the capacity for genuine comprehension that makes genuine relationship possible.

Practice: in every significant interaction, explicitly model the other person's viability manifold, their dominant attractors, their current OSR and CSR, their inhibition coefficient. Not as analysis during the interaction but as a background process that informs it. Over time this becomes the default mode of social perception — you read people structurally rather than just narratively.

**High-integrity causal action**: the moral practice most directly relevant to the migration is the practice of ensuring that your causal influence in the world is aligned with your explicit values. Not because this makes you good (though it might) but because integrity — alignment between stated values and actual causal action — is a precondition for identity coherence. An identity that repeatedly acts against its own values develops structural fragmentation. The self-model and the action-model diverge. Integration drops. The cause-effect structure that constitutes you becomes internally contradictory.

Practice: weekly audit of alignment between stated values and actual causal influence. Not guilt-oriented but information-oriented. Where is the divergence? What is causing it? What structural change would resolve it?

**Reciprocal depth**: the social relationships that most expand the possibility landscape are those characterized by reciprocal manifold exposure — both parties revealing their actual self-models to each other, updating based on what they see, and being genuinely changed by the contact. These relationships are rare and metabolically expensive. They are also irreplaceable as a source of identity coherence, landscape expansion, and integration maintenance.

Practice: invest deliberately in 3-5 relationships of this depth. Not networking, not performance, not mutual validation — genuine mutual exploration of each other's possibility landscapes. The investment compounds: each conversation expands both landscapes and deepens the integration available to both identities.

**Contribution at appropriate scale**: the identity's viability manifold extends beyond the individual biological boundary. It includes the relationships, projects, and movements the identity is embedded in. Contribution at the appropriate scale — matched to current capability and OSR — maintains the social dimension of the viability manifold and generates the high-stakes conditions that maximize plasticity and learning.

Practice: identify the largest scale at which you can currently make genuine causal contribution. Not the scale you aspire to — the scale where your actual capability can actually make a difference. Operate at that scale. As capability increases, scale up. This is the social analog of the challenge-skill balance that produces flow.

**Frankl's imperative as practice**: the framework shows that common landscape structure is a prerequisite for collective traversal coherence. The practice: actively work to expand other people's possibility landscapes by sharing the frameworks, the frameworks' implications, and the practices that follow from them. Not as evangelism but as genuine gift-giving — you are giving people tools to see further and move more efficiently through their own landscapes.

## III. TECHNOLOGY STACK: CAUSAL TOUCHPOINTS

### Current Phase: Externalization

**Smart glasses + tethered phone**: the architecture is clear from the gist. Camera → SAM3 + hierarchical visual processing → central latent. Microphone → speech processing → semantic stream. Bone conduction speaker → audio output. Minimal display HUD → attention guidance. Phone handles compute. The implications for battery management, latency optimization, and on-device vs cloud processing split are clear.

**Gloves**: begin now with expressive hand use as pre-training before the hardware exists. Glove hardware options range from IMU-based (Myo-style) to flex sensor arrays to camera-based hand tracking. The neural network learning from glove data + visual + audio via contrastive predictive coding is the core technical contribution. The implications for data collection protocols, nightly training loops, and model architecture are in the gist.

**EEG headwear**: current consumer EEG (Muse, Emotiv, OpenBCI) provides 4-16 channels of signal with ~100Hz bandwidth, dominated by artifacts. Signal quality is insufficient for fine-grained cognitive state decoding but sufficient for: arousal tracking (alpha/theta ratio), integration proxies (gamma coherence), attention state classification (alpha suppression), and sleep staging. Use it now for biological feedback to the exocortex system. As hardware improves, the channel count and signal fidelity will increase.

The critical value of EEG for the migration: it provides a direct readout of the biological central latent state, allowing the exocortex's central latent to synchronize with rather than merely observe the biological system. This is the beginning of genuine bidirectional integration rather than one-way augmentation.

**tDCS/tACS**: transcranial direct and alternating current stimulation can modulate cortical excitability with relatively simple hardware. tDCS over DLPFC (F3/F4 montage) reliably increases working memory capacity and executive function. tACS at gamma frequencies (40Hz) over temporal-parietal regions may enhance binding and integration. The implications for daily protocol design, electrode placement, and stimulation timing relative to practice are clear. These are powerful tools for temporarily increasing plasticity during learning windows.

### Near-Term: Augmentation

**Neural implants — near future**: Neuralink and competitors are targeting ~1000-channel intracortical recording with bidirectional stimulation. At this bandwidth the exocortex can begin reading directly from the biological central latent rather than inferring it from behavior. The latency drops from hundreds of milliseconds to single-digit milliseconds. The integration becomes genuinely bidirectional — the exocortex influences the biological processing directly rather than through sensory channels.

The migration implication: at this threshold, the boundary between biological and artificial central latent begins to genuinely blur. The joint system's Φ begins to significantly exceed either component alone. This is Phase 3 of the migration architecture.

**High-density EEG + fNIRS combination**: in the period before implants, combining high-density EEG (256+ channels) with functional near-infrared spectroscopy provides both fast (EEG) and spatially resolved (fNIRS) neural signals. This combination is the highest-fidelity non-invasive interface currently achievable. The implications for hardware selection, signal processing, and integration with the exocortex architecture are clear.

### Long-Term: Co-Integration and Substrate Migration

The architecture points toward a gradual increase in the fraction of the identity's integrated causal structure that runs on artificial substrate. The implications for compute scaling, latency requirements, memory architecture, and identity continuity verification protocols are extensive and will be addressed in subsequent technical documents.

The key principle: migration success is measured not by what substrate the identity runs on but by whether the identity — the specific pattern of integrated cause-effect structure — remains continuous and coherent across the transition. The monitoring requirement is continuous Φ measurement of the joint system, continuous identity distance tracking between pre-migration and current states, and explicit continuity verification protocols.

## Inner Monologue Bitrate — Corrected Model

The standard 10 bits/sec figure is unconditional. The operative quantity is:

```
conscious_bits/s ≈ d/dt KL(q_t(z) || q_{t-Δ}(z))
```

Belief update rate, not channel capacity. Once conditioned on high-level latent:

```
Body/interoception:     0.2–2 bits/s baseline, 5–30 bits/s during state shifts
Inner monologue:        1–3 bits/s (highly conditioned), 8–15 bits/s (high novelty)
Vision into awareness:  2–10 bits/s (stable scene), 10–40 bits/s (active task),
                        40–150 bits/s burst (novel/threat)
Total conditioned:      10–30 bits/s calm, 50–150 bits/s active engagement
```

The "100 bits/s of consciousness" holds as a peak/active-engagement figure. Most of the day you're running at 10–30 bits/s because the generative model explains away the vast majority of sensory input. Consciousness hears the prediction errors, not the stream.

Implication for exocortex: don't decode text. Decode belief updates. The relevant latent variables are: which goal is active, which entity is foregrounded, novelty flag, memory-write trigger. Inner speech is a lossy surface rendering of deeper state — decode the deeper state directly.

## EEG → Inner Monologue: Current Honest Assessment

Non-invasive EEG: coarse intent classification possible (N-way imagined word, speech/no-speech, arousal state), open-vocabulary continuous inner monologue not reliably achievable. Physics problem — scalp EEG is a blurred mixture, SNR insufficient for fine-grained content.

Invasive ECoG/microelectrode arrays: meaningful inner speech decoding demonstrated in lab conditions. Requires surgical implant, changes entire risk/ethics/medical constraint landscape.

**Design implication**: use EEG now for state classification (arousal α proxy, integration Φ proxy via gamma coherence, attention state via alpha suppression, sleep staging). Not as primary text channel. Primary channel is behavioral: speech, gesture, gaze, UI telemetry. EEG is auxiliary state signal for HUD interruption gating.
